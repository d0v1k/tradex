Uptrend and Downtrend Pullback Strategy

Objective: Trade NASDAQ (NAS100) by buying on pullbacks during uptrends and selling on pullbacks during downtrends using a 9 EMA and 20 MA with candlestick pattern confirmation.

Confluence Factors:

  • 9 EMA and 20 MA alignment
  • Candlestick Patterns
  • Volume Spikes

Identifying Trends:

  • Uptrend: 9 EMA is above the 20 MA, and both are sloping upwards.
  • Downtrend: 9 EMA is below the 20 MA, and both are sloping downwards.

Entry Criteria:

  • Buying on Pullbacks in Uptrend:
    • Setup: Price pulls back towards the 20 MA while in an uptrend on the 1-hour chart.
    • Confirmation: Look for bullish candlestick patterns (e.g., Hammer, Bullish Engulfing) near the 20 MA on the 15-minute chart.
    • Volume: Ensure there is a volume spike indicating strong buying interest.
    • Entry: Enter a long position when all conditions are met and the price starts to bounce off the 20 MA.
  • Selling on Pullbacks in Downtrend:
    • Setup: Price pulls back towards the 20 MA while in a downtrend on the 1-hour chart.
    • Confirmation: Look for bearish candlestick patterns (e.g., Shooting Star, Bearish Engulfing) near the 20 MA on the 15-minute chart.
    • Volume: Ensure there is a volume spike indicating strong selling interest.
    • Entry: Enter a short position when all conditions are met and the price starts to bounce off the 20 MA.

Exit Criteria:

  • Stop-Loss: Set just beyond the recent swing low (for buy trades) or swing high (for sell trades).
  • Take-Profit: Set at an equal distance to the stop-loss to maintain a 1:1 risk-reward ratio.

Risk Management:

  • Daily Risk: Limit losses to 5% of account balance per day. Stop trading for the day if this limit is reached.
  • Consecutive Losses: Stop trading after three consecutive losing trades to avoid emotional decisions.

Execution Steps:

  • Pre-Session Preparation:
    • Identify the current trend on the 1-hour chart using the 9 EMA and 20 MA.
    • Mark key support and resistance zones on your trading platform.
  • During Trading:
    • Monitor price action around the 20 MA on the 15-minute chart for pullback opportunities.
    • Enter trades when all entry criteria are met.
    • Place stop-loss and take-profit orders immediately upon entering a trade.
  • Post-Trade Review:
    • Log each trade with entry/exit points, reasons for the trade, and outcomes.
    • Review daily to identify patterns, learn from mistakes, and improve strategy.

Example Trade Scenarios:

  • Buying on Pullbacks in Uptrend:
    • Setup: Identify an uptrend with the 9 EMA above the 20 MA, both sloping upwards.
    • Confirmation: Price pulls back to the 20 MA, a Hammer candlestick forms on the 15-minute chart, volume spike confirms buying interest.
    • Entry: Enter long when the price starts to bounce off the 20 MA, set stop-loss below the recent swing low, and take-profit at an equal distance to the stop-loss.
  • Selling on Pullbacks in Downtrend:
    • Setup: Identify a downtrend with the 9 EMA below the 20 MA, both sloping downwards.
    • Confirmation: Price pulls back to the 20 MA, a Shooting Star candlestick forms on the 15-minute chart, volume spike confirms selling interest.
    • Entry: Enter short when the price starts to bounce off the 20 MA, set stop-loss above the recent swing high, and take-profit at an equal distance to the stop-loss.
