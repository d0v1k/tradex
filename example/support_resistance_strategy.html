Support and Resistance Strategy

Objective: Trade NASDAQ (NAS100) using support and resistance zones with confluence factors for higher probability trades.

Confluence Factors:

  • Support and Resistance Zones
  • Candlestick Patterns
  • Volume Spikes

Identifying Key Zones:

  • Use the 1-hour chart with 20/50 MA and volume.
  • Identify zones where price has reversed or stalled multiple times.
  • Draw horizontal zones, not lines.
  • Confirm zones with at least three touches.

Entry Criteria:

  • Support Bounce (Buying):
    • Setup: Price approaches a strong support zone on the 1-hour chart.
    • Confirmation: Look for bullish candlestick patterns (e.g., Hammer, Bullish Engulfing) on the 15-minute chart with 5/13/20 MA and volume.
    • Volume: Ensure there is a volume spike indicating strong buying interest.
    • Entry: Enter a long position when all conditions are met.
  • Resistance Bounce (Selling):
    • Setup: Price approaches a strong resistance zone on the 1-hour chart.
    • Confirmation: Look for bearish candlestick patterns (e.g., Shooting Star, Bearish Engulfing) on the 15-minute chart with 5/13/20 MA and volume.
    • Volume: Ensure there is a volume spike indicating strong selling interest.
    • Entry: Enter a short position when all conditions are met.

Exit Criteria:

  • Stop-Loss: Set just beyond the support (for buy trades) or resistance (for sell trades) zone.
  • Take-Profit: Set at an equal distance to the stop-loss to maintain a 1:1 risk-reward ratio.

Risk Management:

  • Daily Risk: Limit losses to 5% of account balance per day.
  • Consecutive Losses: Stop trading after three consecutive losing trades.

Execution Steps:

  • Pre-Session Preparation:
    • Identify key support and resistance zones on the 1-hour chart.
    • Ensure each identified zone has at least three touches.
    • Mark these zones on your trading platform.
  • During Trading:
    • Monitor price action around these zones on the 15-minute and 5-minute charts.
    • Enter trades when all entry criteria are met.
    • Place stop-loss and take-profit orders immediately upon entering a trade.
  • Post-Trade Review:
    • Log each trade with entry/exit points, reasons for the trade, and outcomes.
    • Review daily to identify patterns, learn from mistakes, and improve strategy.

Example Trade Scenarios:

  • Support Bounce:
    • Setup: Identify a strong support zone at 13,000 on the 1-hour chart.
    • Confirmation: Price approaches 13,000, a Hammer candlestick forms on the 15-minute chart, volume spike confirms buying interest.
    • Entry: Enter long at 13,000, set stop-loss at 12,990, take-profit at 13,010.
  • Resistance Bounce:
    • Setup: Identify a strong resistance zone at 13,500 on the 1-hour chart.
    • Confirmation: Price approaches 13,500, a Shooting Star candlestick forms on the 15-minute chart, volume spike confirms selling interest.
    • Entry: Enter short at 13,500, set stop-loss at 13,510, take-profit at 13,490.
